EventVision: Go To Market Navigation

Introduction

Product Overview

EventVision (EV) is an internal enterprise application used by 500+ internal users to record the scope and cost estimate of a power plant service event.

Users used to set up and disposition financial logistics in an external tool called the Field Services Portal (FSP).

New Go To Market functionality was added to EV in 2021 allowing users to perform the same financial logistics.

Problem Statement

User Acceptance Testing revealed that users struggled to complete the setup workflow introduced in Go To Market.

Ideas for improving the workflow led to conflicts over how to best arrange it for seamless navigation.

Goal

We were challenged to design a second MVP of the Go To Market setup workflow that best accomodated for user behavior.

My Role

1 of 2 UX Designers and Lead UX Researcher

  • information architecture
  • user interviews
  • wireframes and prototyping
  • stakeholder report outs
  • stakeholder management

Timeline

4 sprints (~2 months) before Dev team was scheduled to start building.

Challenges and Limitation

  • User research conducted midway through process after receiving stakeholder permission.
  • Limited user pool due to restricted user access.
  • Back-end system limitations prevented certain solutions.

Business Discovery

Understanding the Business Process

The first step was to meet with business stakeholders to understand the intended business process behind the Go To Market functionality.

During this discussion, I created an information architecture diagram and process map outlining the intended wing to wing process.

The Original Solution

After reviewing the business process, we went over the current solution as well as the different solutions the business proposed to improve the workflow.

During UAT, users struggled to access their Events' Project after Event Setup because it was not in an obvious location. Users would have to scroll down on the left bar to find the link to their Project.

While it was clear that the link to Project should be more readily apparent, stakeholders were split on how to present Project tasks to the users.

The “Split” Solution

The first proposed solution was an iteration of the original, where the Project page would be accessed through a button off to the side of the Event page's tabs.

While this solution made sense from an information hierarchy perspective, there were concerns over the constant back-and-forth users would have to do between Event and Project pages.

The "Combined Solution

The other proposed solution was to embed Project tasks as tabs within the Event page, mirroring the business process workflow. It also closely followed the workflow used in FSP, which some stakeholders believed users would be more familiar with.

While this solution streamlined the workflow, we were concerned that accessing Project tasks, which affect multiple Events, inside the tabset of a single Event would create cause confusion to users.

Additionally, there were doubts that the intended business process was true to how users operated. Namely: how would users know what the Project's financials are if they hadn't built their Events' scopes yet?

User Research

Research Questions

Speaking to end users and understanding their perspective was the key to resolving this conflict. Despite initial pushback from stakeholders, some agreed to let the us interview a small sample of users.

After receiving stakeholder approval, we put together a list of research questions:

  • What stage in the process do you enter POs; before, during, or after you do Scoping work? What factors impact the timing?
  • Do you ever need to go back and make adjustments to your POs?
  • What are your thoughts on EventVision's current workflow/layout for Event and Project?

User Interviews

Our user group spanned 3 major poles: the Americas, Asia, and Europe, and 6 sub-regions: North America, Latin America, Europe, Asia and the Pacific Islands, Middle East and North Africa, and Sub-Saharan Africa.

Ideally, we would have liked to have interviewed at least 1 user from each region, but certain regional stakeholders restricted us from speaking to their users.

We were able to conduct 3 interviews to cover each of the major poles: 1 from North America, 1 from Europe, and 1 from Asia.

Interview Takeaways

From our interviews, we had the following key takeaways:

  • POs needed to be issued as soon as possible to give suppliers ample time to prepare their parts or services. Even if the full Scope of work is not yet finalized, users know enough to begin issuing POs.
  • However, there are times where POs need to be adjusted or new POs need to be issued because of unforeseen circumstances, such as supplier rejections or emergent service work.
  • All users supported separating Event and Project, even wishing that the workflow was driven MORE by Project. In fact, we learned users from Asia were split into groups, where they either worked in Events OR Projects, but not both.

Designs

Research Impacts

Through our interviews, we were able to validate the original business process, and confirm that users do issue POs before doing Scope or Cost.

Based on this confirmation and the users' need to adjust POs in the middle of planning, we determined that accessing Project through a button off to the side, while an improvement on the original, was not ideal considering the frequent back-and-forth.

We also learned that users wished the workflow was driven more by Projects than Events, so we determined that consolidating the workflow within an Event was also not ideal.

Competitive Analysis

Seeing as both proposed solutions were not ideal for users, we decided to look to our sister product, FieldVision and see how they were arranging their navigation.

Their solution for navigation across different areas of the app was to use dropdown menu navigation.

In addition to being a solution to our problem, there were also plans to eventually merge FieldVision and EventVision together. As such, we decided that mirroring FieldVision's solution could be the ideal solution for both the short and long term.

Updated Solution

Dropdown menu navigation would provide our users the division between Event and Project they needed, while also keeping the navigation between the two simpler and more flexible.

We would also be able to easily combine our menus with FieldVision's menus whenever the two products to become one.

Additionally, we included a separate persistent dropdown to the right, which allows users to more easily navigate to any sister Event within their Project or Event.

Initial Results and Next Steps

Initial Results

Stakeholders were impressed with how we were able to create a solution that combined the advantages of the two original proposed solutions.

The improved solution also encouraged some stakeholders to let us speak with more of their end users for future interviews.

Next Steps

Our next steps are to conduct usability testing on our updated solution with more end users and iterate the navigation based on their feedback.

Once navigation is finalized, we will also begin working through improving the individual tasks within the Go To Market functionality.

Hypothetical Adjustments

Research Adjustments

Ideally, if we had the time and proper access, I would have liked to interview multiple users from each region to make sure we had a complete understanding of our userbase and their regional differences.

Additionally, with a larger user population, we could have done A/B Testing for the original two solutions proposed.

Solution Adjustments

Given the Parent-Child relationship Projects have with Events, it seemed counterintuitive to me to start the workflow with Events.

However, the back-end system requires Projects be seeded from Events, a relic of FSP, and overhauling this system would take too much of our developers' limited time.
